Pork Sinigang

Photo by Joy D. Ganaden

A classic Filipino sour soup made with tender pork, fresh vegetables, and a tamarind based broth. It is warm, comforting, and perfect for rainy days or family meals.

This recipe uses pork belly for a rich and flavorful broth. The sour taste comes from tamarind, which gives the soup its signature tangy flavor. Fresh vegetables like radish, eggplant, and water spinach add texture and balance to the savory pork.

The key to a delicious sinigang is slow simmering. Cooking the pork gently makes it soft and allows the flavors to blend well. The vegetables are added at the right time so they stay tender but not overcooked, giving you a hearty and satisfying dish.

Ingredients

  • 1kg pork belly, cut into chunks
  • 8 cups water
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 pack, about 40 grams, sinigang mix or 1 cup fresh tamarind pulp
  • 1 medium radish, sliced
  • 1 medium eggplant, sliced
  • 2 cups water spinach, kangkong
  • 2 pieces green chili
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • Salt to taste

Steps in Cooking

  1. In a large pot, boil 8 cups of water. Add the pork belly and cook for 40 to 60 minutes until tender. Remove any foam that rises to the top.
  2. Add the onion and tomatoes. Simmer for 5 minutes until softened.
  3. Stir in the sinigang mix or tamarind pulp. Mix well until fully dissolved.
  4. Add the radish and cook for 3 to 5 minutes.
  5. Add the eggplant and green chili. Cook for another 5 minutes.
  6. Season with fish sauce and salt according to your taste.
  7. Add the water spinach last and cook for 1 to 2 minutes.
  8. Turn off the heat and serve hot with steamed rice.
